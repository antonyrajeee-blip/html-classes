    Facts about the T-rex

    The T-rex was one of the largest land carnivores of all time. It lived during the late Cretaceous period, around 68 to 66 million years ago. The T-rex had a massive skull, powerful jaws, and sharp teeth that could crush bones. It was a bipedal dinosaur, meaning it walked on two legs, and it had small arms with two fingers. Despite its size and strength, the T-rex is believed to have been a fast runner, capable of reaching speeds of up to 20 miles per hour.

    The T-rex is one of the most well-known dinosaurs and has been featured in numerous movies, books, and documentaries. It continues to captivate the imagination of people around the world and remains a symbol of prehistoric power and ferocity.
